Campus Access

Accessible routes, transport, housing, and inclusive events across Scottish universities

All Scottish campuses must be accessible

Under the Equality Act 2010 and Scottish public sector duties, every university has accessible entrances, lifts, toilets, and hearing loops.

Standard Accessible Facilities (All Scottish Universities)

  • Step-free access to all teaching buildings
  • Automatic doors and ramps
  • Accessible toilets on every floor
  • Lifts with audio announcements and Braille
  • Hearing loops in lecture theatres
  • Quiet/refuge spaces
  • Priority seating in classrooms
  • Accessible library study carrels
  • Height-adjustable desks available on request
  • Blue Badge parking near main entrances

Getting To & Around Campus

Campus Shuttles & Buses

Most universities run wheelchair-accessible shuttles (free or low cost). Lothian Buses, First Bus, and Stagecoach in Scotland have low-floor buses with ramps.

Lothian Buses Accessibility

Trains & ScotRail

All ScotRail trains have wheelchair spaces and priority seating. Book assistance 24h in advance.

ScotRail Accessible Travel
Phone: 0800 912 2901

Taxis & Adapted Vehicles

Use Wheelchair Accessible Vehicles (WAVs) in all major cities. Many universities subsidise taxi costs via DSA.

Accessible University Accommodation

Adapted Rooms

Every Scottish university offers adapted bedrooms with:

  • Wider doors & turning space
  • Level-access/wet-room showers
  • Emergency pull cords
  • Vibrating pillows & flashing doorbells (deaf students)
Apply early — priority deadline usually March/April

Live-in Support

DSA can fund overnight support workers if needed. Some residences allow assistance dogs.

Organising Inclusive Events

All university events must be accessible. Quick checklist:

  • Wheelchair-accessible venue
  • BSL interpreter if requested
  • Live captioning for presentations
  • Microphones (no exceptions)
  • Accessible slides (high contrast, alt text)
  • Quiet space available
