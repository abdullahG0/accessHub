“Inclusion is not bringing people into what already exists — it is making a new space where everyone belongs.” — Scottish Disability Equality Forum

Disability is Part of Human Diversity

1 in 5 Students

Identify as disabled — including invisible conditions like dyslexia, ADHD, autism, and mental health.

90% of Disabilities

Are not visible. Never assume someone “doesn’t look disabled”.

Inclusion Benefits Everyone

Captions help non-native speakers. Flexible deadlines help parents. Accessible design is better design.

How to Be a Disability Ally in Scotland

  • Ask before helping — “Would you like assistance?”
  • Respect chosen language — some prefer “disabled person”, others “person with disability”
  • Use microphones in every meeting — helps deaf/hard-of-hearing students
  • Provide slides and materials in advance
  • Promote BSL inclusion — book interpreters when needed
  • Challenge ableist language — “crazy”, “lame”, “blind leading the blind”
  • Believe people when they disclose a disability
  • Make accessibility the default — not an afterthought
