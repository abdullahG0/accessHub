About Equal Access Scotland

Your independent, student-centred guide to disability support and inclusion across every university and college in Scotland.

Our Mission

We believe every disabled student in Scotland deserves to study, thrive and graduate on equal terms — without having to fight for the support they’re legally entitled to.

Equal Access Scotland was created by former disabled students and accessibility advisors who saw the same barriers again and again. We’ve turned that experience into a free, trusted, Scotland-specific resource that puts the information you need in one place.

No jargon. No dead-end links. Just clear, up-to-date guidance on DSA funding, assistive technology, campus access, legal rights and real student stories.

How We Help Scottish Students

Your Legal Rights in Scotland

Equality Act 2010

Universities must make “reasonable adjustments” and cannot discriminate.

British Sign Language (Scotland) Act 2015

Public bodies (including universities) must publish BSL plans.

Disabled Students’ Allowance (DSA)

Up to £27,000+ per year for equipment, software and human support (via SAAS for Scottish-domiciled students).
