Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to tell the university I’m disabled?

No — disclosure is entirely voluntary. You only need to tell your disability adviser to get support. Your lecturers only see your adjustments (e.g. “extra exam time”), not your diagnosis.

When should I apply for DSA?

As early as possible! You can apply as soon as you have an offer. There is no deadline, but it takes 6–12 weeks to process.

Will DSA affect my other student finance?

No — DSA is non-means-tested and non-repayable. It does not count as income and won’t reduce your maintenance loan.

Can I get DSA if I already have a degree?

Yes — postgraduates and second-degree students can still get DSA (except for PGCE/ITTs which have different funding).

Do I need a formal diagnosis?

Yes, for most conditions. A GP letter, diagnostic report, or consultant letter is usually enough. Dyslexia assessments from university are accepted.

Can I get help with travel costs?

Yes — DSA can pay taxi fares if public transport is inaccessible due to your disability.

Is mental health considered a disability?

Yes — anxiety, depression, bipolar, OCD, etc. all count under the Equality Act if they have a substantial, long-term impact.

Can I get a laptop through DSA?

Yes — most students receive a high-spec laptop + printer + software bundle.

What if my needs change during my course?

Contact your university disability team — you can have another needs assessment and get extra funding.

Do part-time students get DSA?

Yes — as long as you’re studying at least 25% of a full-time course.

Can I use DSA for accommodation costs?

DSA doesn’t cover rent, but universities must provide accessible housing at the same price band.

Who pays for BSL interpreters?

Your university — not you. They can reclaim costs via their funding council.

What if I’m refused adjustments?

Contact your disability adviser first. If still refused, you can make a formal complaint or contact the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman.
